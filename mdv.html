About the Maldives


Averaging just 1.5m above sea level, seal level rise poses an existential threat to The Maldives. It is also one of the most dispersed countries in the world with just 298km2 of land area spread over 929,000km2 of the Indian ocean. The situation facing the Maldives is to a large extent spatial and therefore architectural.


1200+ unique islands
450,000 people
200 Inhabited islands

Due to its geology and dispersed area, the geography and demographics of the country are constantly in flux. In the field of architecture the first step of proposing a solution is understanding the situation. Mapping the country in a way that captures its ever changing nature is crucial for the appropriate use of design.

Sea level rise

The largest international studies on the effects of climate change predict between 0.3m and 2.5m of sea level rise by 2100. With the extent of the changes unknown, rigid plans for the future are a risky strategy. We postulate at 4D that intelligent incremental design solutions are preferable to minimise risk and maximise the chance for positive outcomes.

Each island faces unique climate change threats

Every island in the archipelago is different not only spatially but in terms of inhabitation patterns and its relationship to local and global weather systems. We believe unique situations should ideally have unique design solutions. This process can be greatly aided by digital techniques which allow portions of the analysis and problem solving required to be automated.

With no action the country could only have 100 years left...

New design approach is required to adapt to the changing enviroment

We must think differently to address these huge questions positively. Both the outcome and the process of design must be adaptable and open to unexpected change. Ultimately design that embraces change has the opportunity to be both more natural and more human.

As highlighted by the

Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change:

“Community-based adaptation has been shown to generate larger benefits when delivered in conjunction with other development activities. Recent moves towards participatory approaches that link scientific knowledge with local visions of vulnerability offer an important way forward to understanding island vulnerability in the absence of certainty in model-based scenarios.”
IPCC, AR5 report, Small Island Developing States, Chpt 29

4D Island is currently investigating these issue in the maldives with the aim to develop and open source platform for sustainable development.
